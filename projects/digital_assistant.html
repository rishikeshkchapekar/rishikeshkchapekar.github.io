About VirtualAssistant

VirtualAssist was a Digital Assistant which was capable of doing small tasks such as sending emails,setting reminders,controlling appliances etc

It ran on a Raspberry Pi, thus makin it a Google Home or Alexa style hardware based product

It used ML to understand speech and interpret the command from it

Features

Speech Recognition:

The bot used Google's Speech Recognition to convert speech to text.Many other systems were considered, with preference to offline speech recognition. However, offline ones were not as accurate, hence Google's speech recognizer was finally selected

Understanding Commands:

The intial version of the assistant searched for keywords (in a specific order for certain commands) and executed functions accordingly. Later an ML model was trained to understand the context of the user's speech

Voice Input:

Initial versions took every word spoken into account for deciding which functions to execute, and soon upgraded to responding only after a trigger word

Commands:

Internet Search: Get information on any topic from the internet, by asking it to search for specific keywords

Get Weather Reports: Its database had a section of city names, covering almost every city on Earth, and a weather API to get a weather report of that city

Reminders: The assistant could remember anything the user wanted it to remember and the stored notes could be accessed by simply asking it to list out all reminders.

Schedule Events: You could also store an event such as an alarm, or any event ask it to remind you at a particular time

Play Music: Asking it to simply play music would play a song at random from the system storage. Asking it to play a specific song would search the system for that song,and if not found,play it via YouTube

Sending Emails: You could ask it to send an email to someone, if the name didn't exist in its database, it would ask you to create a new entry by asking the name and email ID of the recipient

Control Appliances: Though not initated completely, test cases were built. A relay board was used with a WiFi compatible microcontroller which would recieve wireless signals from the Raspberry Pi on the user's command, and control appliances such as lights,fans etc.
