About Scheduler

Scheduler was part of a larger home automation system, built in 2017

A WiFi enabled microcontroller was connected to the main switchboard through a series of relays

An access point was provided over a newtwork served by the microcontroller itself, which was used to set the custom schedules

Working

Concept Of The Scheduler:

The main concept was to automate the process of switching on and off the appliances which were being used at specific times every day, such as porch lights after sundown,switching off appliances in certain rooms at a particular time at night etc.

The user could set a custom schedule for each day, and the scheduler would control the mentioned appliances at the given times

Design:

Hardware: An ESP8266 Microcontroller was used,connected to an array of relay modules which were responsible to bear the actual load,thus keeping the microcontroller,as well as the appliances safe.

Accessing The Scheduler: The ESP8266 served its own local network with a fixed IP address for itself,which could be accessed from any device on the same network (an admin login-password was needed to get to the actual schedule page

Setting The Schedule: Once logged in on the schedules page,the user could see a list of all appliances associated with the scheduler, followed by a table of days of the week and time slots.

Further Development: Although never implemented, a concept was built which learnt the user's usual schedule of switching on and off appliances, and automatically create a schedule for him/her
